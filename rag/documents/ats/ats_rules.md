# ATS Evaluation Rules and Scoring Criteria

## Scoring Breakdown (Total: 100 points)

### 1. Skill Match (50 points max)
- 90–100% of required skills matched → 45–50 points
- 70–89% of required skills matched → 35–44 points
- 50–69% of required skills matched → 25–34 points
- 30–49% of required skills matched → 15–24 points
- Below 30% of required skills matched → 0–14 points

### 2. Relevant Work Experience (25 points max)
- 3+ years in directly relevant roles → 20–25 points
- 1–3 years in relevant roles → 12–19 points
- Less than 1 year or tangential experience → 5–11 points
- No relevant experience → 0–4 points

### 3. Projects with Quantified Impact (15 points max)
- 3+ projects with measurable outcomes (e.g., "reduced load time by 40%") → 12–15 points
- 1–2 projects with some measurable outcomes → 7–11 points
- Projects listed but no measurable impact → 3–6 points
- No relevant projects → 0–2 points

### 4. Education Relevance (10 points max)
- Degree directly relevant to role (CS, Engineering, etc.) + certifications → 8–10 points
- Relevant degree, no certifications → 5–7 points
- Unrelated degree but certifications → 3–4 points
- No relevant education or certifications → 0–2 points

## Calibration Examples
- A junior candidate with 2–3 matched skills and no experience: score 20–35
- A mid-level candidate with most skills and 2 years experience: score 55–70
- A senior candidate with all required skills and 5+ years experience: score 80–95

## General Rules
- Do NOT give a score of exactly 60 unless the candidate genuinely fits that range
- Score must reflect THIS specific resume vs THIS specific role
- Missing core technical skills must significantly reduce the score
- Strong project evidence can compensate for missing formal experience
- Always prefer specificity over a generic middle estimate
